What is fire?


Fire has been discovered over 500,000 years ago and has been used ever since. It can the reason why humans are still alive to this day. Though fire plays a vital role in our lives, It can be severely dangerous if you start one on accident and are unable to control it. Fire happens when there is a chemical reaction that converts fuel and oxygen into carbon dioxide and water. This chemical reaction happens when 2 gases meet each other and react and react accordingly depending on how many chemicals there are. Fire produces light and energy which plays a vital role in our survival, though if the fire is uncontrolled, the toxic fumes that it can make are enough to kill a human. Furthermore, the heat it produces can severely burn a human and can lead to death.



What is fire used for?

The main use of fire throughout history is to cook food. Fire produces heat which can efficiently make food without taking that much time. Fire is one of the reasons we are alive since we are able to cook raw meat and not get diseases from them. In countries with harsh winters, Fire is used to keep them warm. People are able to survive through the winter for several decades now because fire is able to produce heat which keeps them warm. Fire is also produced to generate electricity. Without fire, almost all of the power plants will be unable to produce electricity and that can have a major impact on the economy. Fire or flame is also used to light up our surroundings. Fire made it possible for the efficient production of electricity, which made it possible for us to power our countries with light. With light, we are able to work throughout the night and are able to explore dark parts of the world without having the fear of not knowing where to go.
